Fertility Rate

As can be seen:

- A positive correlation with higher fertility rates leading to higher population growth rates

- More births contribute to an increase in population

- Majority of the African countries have a higher population growth and fertility rates

- Most populous countries have a low to medium range of fertility rates. Could be due to urbanisation, higher cost of living or better education

- Outliers consists of: Ukraine and Moldova due to ongoing conflicts, and Syria due to return of refugees

~~~

Infant Mortality (per 1000 births)

As can be seen:

- A positive correlation with higher infant mortality leading to higher population growth rates

- Due to response to high infant mortality and lack of family planning resources

- Majority of the African countries have a higher population growth and infant mortality

- Most populous countries have a low to medium range of infant mortality. Could be due better healthcare and education for these economically developed countries

- Outliers consists of: Ukraine and Moldova due to ongoing conflicts, and Syria due to return of refugees

~~~

Life Expectancy

As can be seen:

- A negative correlation with lower life expectancy leading to higher population growth rates

- Better economically developed countries provide better healthcare access but also to high costs of raising children

- Majority of the African countries have a higher population growth but lower life expectancy

- Majority of the European countries have a lower population growth but higher life expectancy

- Most populous countries have a medium range of life expectancy.

- Outliers consists of: Ukraine due to ongoing conflicts, and Syria due to return of refugees
